Allama Muhammad Iqbal

Introduction

Allama Muhammad Iqbal (1877 - 1938) was a Muslim philosopher, poet, politician, and scholar who is considered one of the most important figures in Urdu and Persian literature

Poetry

A first collection of poetry, written in Persian, was published in 1915 with the title Asrar-e-Khudi, the English translation being Secrets of the Self. He produced many more in the Persian language as well as some in Urdu, such as Bang-e-Dara (The Call of the Marching Bell), and Zarb-i-Kalim.

death

Allama Muhammad Iqbal died from a mysterious throat illness in Lahore on April 21, 1938 after suffering for months: Illness Iqbal suffered from a mysterious throat illness for months before his death. Last days In his final years, Iqbal often visited the Dargah of Ali Hujwiri in Lahore for spiritual guidance. Death It is said that Iqbal breathed his last while listening to a kafi of Bulleh Shah. Iq
